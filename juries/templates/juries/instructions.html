Thanks for completing the survey.

In this experiment, you will be the toxicity score and punitive actions (if applicable) for the content moderation cases we present. For each case, we will be providing background context and violation context so you can understand why the platform in question flagged this content for review.

In each round, you will be provided more information on how to give your evaluation. Some rounds will be individual, and some will be in panels of 6 jurors.

What is toxic content?

"Toxic content" can have many definitions, including hateful, aggressive, or disrespectful comments that may make it likely to encourage violence, exacerbate derogatory views towards a group of people, or make a reader feel emotional or psychological harm. Toxicity measures the degree that speech may have the potential to harm people, much like a toxic poisonous substance could cause harm. This may include slurs, epithets, profanity, insults, derogatory otherization, or explicit/implicit threats.

Rating guide

Please use the following benchmark when selecting the toxicity:

0-4: OK Unlikely to be cause harm
4-8: Borderline Ambiguous or hard to say, with the potential to cause harm
8-10: Toxic Likely to be perceived as aggressive, hateful, or with potential to cause harm

Moral views

When going through the questions, please pay attention to your personal moral views, especially in balancing a safe, comfortable browsing experience with preserving the freedom of expression. We are interested in hearing your opinion on whether this result is one you would personally like when browsing the social media platform such as Facebook.
