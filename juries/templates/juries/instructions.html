Instructions

What you'll be doing

In this experiment, you will be rating content for:

  1. Toxicity score
  2. Punitive actions on the content (if applicable)
  3. Punitive actions on the user (if applicable)

For each of the 3 cases, we will be providing background context and violation context so you can understand why the platform in question flagged this content for review.

Jury Interface

In each round, you will be provided more information on how to give your evaluation. Some rounds will be individual, and some will be in panels of 6 jurors. The decision panel will be at the bottom of the page in each of the three rounds.

What is toxic content?

"Toxic content" can have many definitions, including hateful, aggressive, or disrespectful comments that may make it likely to encourage violence, exacerbate derogatory views towards a group of people, or make a reader feel emotional or psychological harm. Toxicity measures the degree that speech may have the potential to harm people, much like a toxic poisonous substance could cause harm. This may include slurs, epithets, profanity, insults, derogatory otherization, or explicit/implicit threats.

How to rate Toxicity Score

Regardless of what you think should be done with the content, please use the following benchmark to select your personal opinion on how toxic the content is:

  • 0-3: OK, unlikely to be cause harm
  • 4-7: Borderline, ambiguous or hard to say, with the potential to cause harm
  • 8-10: Toxic, likely to be perceived as aggressive, hateful, or with potential to cause harm

How to rate Punitive Actions

The platforms (social media companies like Facebook, Twitter, Reddit, etc.) have certain powers to control what content remain on their sites and what rights are given to the users of their sites. When suggesting punitive actions for toxic content, please select all that apply for what response you think it requires.

Content Actions

  • Unlist from search: Still available via direct links, but not automatically pushed to users' feeds
  • Delete: Removed from the site, unaccessible by anyone
  • Report to Authorities: Removed from the site and forwarded to local authorities (e.g. police, child protection agencies)

User Actions

  • Warn: Issue a written warning for users advising them of the potential harms of their post
  • Ban for 1 week: "Temporary timeout" for users as punishment
  • Permanently ban: Permanently remove user from site, block email and IP address

Personal views

When going through the questions, please pay attention to your personal moral views on whether this result is one you would personally like when browsing the social media platform such as Facebook. We are interested in studying how users balance preserving freedom of expression while maintaining a safe, comfortable browsing experience.
